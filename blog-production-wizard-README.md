# Blog Production Wizard — How to Access & Use It

A self-contained checklist that walks you through publishing one weekly blog post, start to finish. It's a single HTML file — no install, no account, no internet required (except to open the tool links and load the Jost brand font).

---

## 1. Save it somewhere permanent

Move `blog-production-wizard.html` out of your Downloads folder and into a spot you'll keep it — for example, a `Blog Workflow` folder, or your OneDrive so it's backed up. The wizard remembers your progress in whatever browser you open it from, so once you pick a home for it, keep opening it the same way.

## 2. Open it

Double-click the file. It opens in your default browser.

**Use Chrome or Edge.** The "Copy the readings into your Learning Design Knowledgebase folder" step uses a browser feature that only those two support. In other browsers that step still works as a checkbox — you'd just move the files yourself.

## 3. Make it one-click to reach (pick whichever you like)

- **Bookmark it:** with the file open, bookmark the page (Ctrl+D). It'll save the `file://…` location so you can reopen it from your bookmarks bar.
- **Desktop shortcut:** right-click the file → *Send to → Desktop (create shortcut)* on Windows, or drag it to the dock/desktop on Mac.
- **Pin the browser tab:** right-click the open tab → *Pin*.

## 4. Split-screen while you work

Snap the wizard to one side of your screen and your work to the other:
- **Windows:** click the wizard window, press `Win + ←`.
- **Mac:** hover the green window button → *Tile Window to Left of Screen*.

---

## How it works

- **Twelve steps, one at a time.** Each screen shows the action, any tool link, reference details, and to-dos.
- **Mark complete** advances you; **Back** returns to the previous step to revisit it. Completed steps show a *Complete* badge with an **Unmark** option.
- **It saves automatically.** Close the tab mid-process and your checkmarks, notes, week number, and thesis are still there when you reopen — as long as it's the same browser.
- **Click-to-copy is everywhere.** The blog-post prompt, the per-output social prompts, your notes, and filenames all copy with one click so you can paste straight into Claude, ChatGPT, or NotebookLM.
- **Start new week** (top-right, or on the finish screen) clears your notes, thesis, and checkmarks for a fresh run. It asks first.

## The flow at a glance

1. **Save readings from Canvas**
2. **Read the articles & take notes** — capture Topics to Explore + reading notes; upload to the Claude project; copy readings to your Knowledgebase folder
3. **Set up the post** — enter week #, blog post #, and thesis (check your blog for the post number)
4. **Write the blog post** — copy the full prompt, paste into your Claude desktop project, new chat
5. **Generate images in ChatGPT**
6. **Compress the images** (TinyPNG)
7. **Record the voiceover** (ElevenLabs)
8. **Generate social files** (NotebookLM) — pick the outputs you want; each has its own copyable prompt
9. **Compress the videos** (HandBrake)
10. **Edit the videos** (Premiere)
11. **Publish the blog post** (Wix)
12. **Post to social media** — Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn

---

## Good to know

- **Progress lives in one browser on one device.** It doesn't sync between, say, your laptop and phone. Use the same browser each week and you'll be fine.
- **The deep-dive audio podcast** is intentionally generated from the source articles + your thesis (not your blog post), so it stays general and advice-focused, without personal detail. The wizard's prompt for that output enforces it.
- **Facebook and Instagram count as one** since you have them linked to auto-share.
- If you ever want this on your phone with automatic weekly recurrence instead, the same checklist can live as a recurring task in ClickUp or Asana — just ask.
